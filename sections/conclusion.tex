\reportsection{Conclusion and Next Steps}
\label{sec:conclusion}

A vector commitment is only as strong as the parameters underneath it, and the
default \textsf{ark-mt} configurations miss that target for small fields on both
axes at once.  The parametrisation model fixes the boundary at the reference
level, derives the parameters a target forces, and reports the evidence: the
binding bound, its birthday step, and the BabyBear sizing carry a machine-checked
Lean backing, the salt-cardinality lemmas are certified, and the typed hiding
interface is implemented, so binding holds end to end while hiding has its
prerequisites in place for the rest.

\paragraph{Next.}
Four obligations remain open: the random-oracle hiding composition that lifts the
certified salt sizes to a selective-opening theorem; the extraction bridge
\code{cacheExtract\_sound}, currently a \code{sorry}; multi-round BCS and
Fiat--Shamir soundness, which justifies the strengthened digest target; and the
non-binary shapes, which share the generic binding reduction but are not yet
exhibited.
